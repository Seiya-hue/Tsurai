Data, Information, & Information Systems

Data refers to raw facts and figures that are collected, processed, and stored. It is the foundation upon which information is built.
Information is data that has been given meaning and context, making it useful for decision-making.
Types of Support Systems.

Key Concepts in Data and Information:

Data vs. Information: Data is the raw material, while information is the processed and meaningful output.

Data Quality: The accuracy, completeness, consistency, and timeliness of data are crucial for its usefulness.

Information Overload: The excessive amount of information can hinder decision-making and lead to information fatigue.

Knowledge Management: The process of identifying, capturing, organizing, and sharing information to improve decision-making and organizational performance.

Key Components in Information Systems:

Input: Collecting data from various sources, such as sensors, databases, and user input.

Processing: Manipulating and transforming data into useful information through calculations, analysis, and decision-making processes.

Storage: Storing data and information for future reference and retrieval.

Output: Presenting information in a meaningful format, such as reports, graphs, or visualizations.

Types of Support Sytems:

Transaction Processing Systems (TPS): Handle routine business activities like sales, inventory, and payroll.

Management Information Systems (MIS): Provide summarized information to managers for decision-making.

Decision Support Systems (DSS): Offer tools and techniques to analyze data and support decision-making.

Executive Information Systems (EIS): Provide high-level summaries to top executives for strategic planning.

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP): Integrate various business functions to improve efficiency and coordination.
